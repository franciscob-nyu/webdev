The Ways of Seeing by John Berger explores how we see and interpret visual images in art and popular culture.
Berger argues that our cultural and social contexts shape our vision and understanding of the world.
He examines various aspects of visual culture, critiques the Western art canon,
and shows how visual images reflect and reinforce dominant social and economic structures.
It challenges the idea that seeing is a neutral and objective process, and highlights the ways in which
our biases and experiences influence our perception of the world.
